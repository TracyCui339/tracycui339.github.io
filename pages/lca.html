Understanding Life Cycle Assessment

Comprehensive LCA Overview

Explore the methodology, standards, and applications of Life Cycle Assessment in maritime sustainability.

What is LCA

LCA is a systematic methodology used to evaluate the environmental impacts associated with all stages of a product's life cycle.

Raw Material Acquisition

Type and source of raw materials used for production (e.g., agricultural feedstocks, algae)

Production

Production process details including process energy inputs and outputs

Transportation & Distribution

Modes of transport used to distribute to the locations

Use Phase

Specific fuel oil consumption rates under different operating conditions

End-of-Life and Disposal

Disposal methods for any waste products or by-products from the use phase

International standards and guidelines that govern LCA methodology

LCA Analysis

Understanding different scopes of emissions in LCA

SAMPLE 1

Comparative Analysis – Compared with a baseline
  • Identify percentage differences based on its emission from each life cycle stage
  • Identify advantages and disadvantages (operational, etc)
  • Example: B100 vs MGO

SAMPLE 2

Sensitivity Analysis – Identify hotspots
  • Identify and address any significant emission contribution within the life cycle stage
  • Assess for any feasible measures
  • Example: LCA for ammonia is most significant at the production stage

SAMPLE 3

Environmental Analysis - Life Cycle Impact Assessment
  • Identification of midpoint impact categories and endpoint area of protection
  • Assess significant areas of impact to be mitigated and cautioned
  • Example: Human toxicity and ozone depletion
